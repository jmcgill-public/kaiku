# comp

Scores, notes, and prose from the universe.

No DSP language here. This is not the technical surface — that is `arch/`. This is where the music lives as music: notation, seasonal structure, compositional decisions, the record context.

---

## the tool

The thirteen-minute form is not an aesthetic choice about duration. It is a behavioral tool: something to sit down with and quietly listen to for thirteen minutes.

This is harder than it sounds. The compositions do not reward impatience. They do not summarize themselves. They do not build to a resolution you can leave on. The drone is there when you arrive and it is there when you leave. The seasonal arc moves through its proportions regardless of whether you are following it. The thirteen minutes is the prescription, not the runtime.

Patent pending.

## what this silence is not

John Cage's 4'33" (1952): the performer sits. Nothing is played. The ambient sounds of the room become the music. The silence is a frame; what fills it is the piece. Cage's argument: there is no silence, only sounds you weren't attending to. The composition is the act of attending.

That is not this.

The silence in these compositions is not a frame for the ambient world. The drone is always present — it does not stop. Hiljaisuus is not the absence of sound. It is a specific thing: Station Nine, a place, a presence. The silence here has content. It is not waiting to be filled by the room.

Sitting with this for thirteen minutes is not Cage's practice of attention to ambient sound. It is something else. The name for what it is has not been decided.

## register

Everything in `comp/` is written in the composition register. The prose is about the music, not about how the music was made. Tempo, key, form, seasonal proportion, character. Not signal chains, not parameter values.

When a compositional decision has a technical consequence, note the consequence briefly and reference `arch/` for the mechanism.

---

## the record

One record. Two tracks. Side A / Side B. 12-inch vinyl, 1982 standard.

The physical format is the constraint and the aesthetic. The limitations are the vocabulary.

**Side A** — the primary track. ~4:35. F# Phrygian. Tyhjyydenkaiku enters in section 3.

**Side B** — *Toinen raita* (working title). The koivuhuilu duo. Two flutes in thirds, descending through winter. The inner groove position is planned for.

Score documents for both tracks belong here.
