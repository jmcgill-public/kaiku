## Structure

```
1. HILJAISUUS          — The silence opens. The IR space. The pulse.
                         Nothing asks you to begin.

2. CONDENSATION        — The silence becomes rhythm. Syncopated, uptempo.
                         The void has a tempo. Nobody told you that.

3. HURDY ENTERS        — Tyhjyydenkaiku lead. The instrument announced.
                         The melody over the syncopated bed.

4. SYNTHETIC CHORUS    — FM mouth music voices. Lausuja and Kaiku.
                         Toch-style rhythmic counterpoint, Phrygian field.
                         The machine choir that learned what mouths do.

5. SUNG VERSE          — Human voice. Finnish, phonetically trained.
                         The worldborn singing in the void's language.
                         One verse. No more.

6. KUOLINVIHELLYS      — Selli's death whistle. The interruption.
                         The threshold. The break in the surface.
                         Uninvited. Not long. Doesn't need to be.

7. KOIVUHUILU          — Birch flute. Sweet, sanguine, descending.
                         The forest IR under it (Kaipuus, not built yet).
                         Drawing down. Into the void. Gently.
                         The void was always this gentle.
```

---

## Structural note

This is the Reko/Turo/Keijo structure in music.

- Hiljaisuus + condensation: **Reko** — the job, the surface, the rhythm
- Hurdy + chorus: **Turo** — the pattern, the language beginning to resolve
- Death whistle: **Keijo** — the break, the understanding, the cost
- Birch flute: **aftermath** — what you carry back down

The player doesn't change. The listener gets closer.

## Technical requirements by section

**Section 1 — Hiljaisuus**
- Convolution space: hiljaisuus_station.wav dominant
- Hiljaisuus pulse: slow AM on a near-sine tone, void-violet register
- Duration: long enough that the listener adjusts. Don't rush it.

**Section 2 — Condensation**
- Rhythm emerges FROM the texture — not a new section, a crystallization
- Geographical Fugue logic: speech-rhythm or percussive FM entering in imitation
- Syncopated: the pulse is felt before it's stated
- Uptempo relative to section 1 — the contrast is the point

**Section 3 — Hurdy**
- Tyhjyydenkaiku primary patch
- F# Phrygian lead
- Station IR at 20-30% wet
- The trompette (Layer C) on accented notes only

**Section 4 — Chorus**
- FM mouth music voices (Timbre 1 from timbre_brief.md)
- Layered: Lausuja leads, Kaiku echoes at 2-bar delay
- Ilma (sustained soprano) above
- Pohja drone underneath throughout
- Geographical Fugue entry logic: voices stack in

**Section 5 — Sung verse**
- Human voice, Finnish, phonetically trained
- One verse only — the machine has been speaking, now a person speaks
- Melody: narrow range, Kalevala trochaic meter if possible
- Finnish phonetics brief needed (to be developed separately)
- Station IR under voice: same room, you are in the station

**Section 6 — Kuolinvihellys**
- Death whistle registration (to be built)
- Short. An interruption, not a movement.
- The purple IR eventually — for now: dry or with station IR at low wet
- The flute stops. The whistle doesn't announce itself.

**Section 7 — Koivuhuilu**
- Birch flute registration (to be built)
- Kaipuus forest IR (to be built)
- Descending phrase — F# Phrygian resolving downward
- Sweet, sanguine: warm breath, slight flatness, wood grain in the tone
- Fade into the void. Not a hard ending.
