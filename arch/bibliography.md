# bibliography

Formation, lineage, and theoretical reference for the instrument and its sound.

This is not an exhaustive list. It is the load-bearing entries — the works that made decisions about what Kaiku is and is not.

---

## formation

**Barron, Louis and Bebe.** *Forbidden Planet* — electronic tonalities (1956). MGM.

The first all-electronic film score. Built custom circuits for each sound. The circuits were organisms: they had lifespans. They burned out. The sounds cannot be reproduced because the instruments no longer exist. The deterioration was not a failure of the process — it was the process. The imperfection is the instrument.

The 8-cent detuning in Tyhjyydenkaiku is in this lineage. So is the decision not to expose it as a parameter.

---

**Scott, Raymond.** The Electronium (1959–c.1987, unfinished).

The machine as composer. Not a synthesizer you play — a device that generates music autonomously. Scott worked on it from 1959 until his stroke in 1987. He considered it unfinished at his death. It was never wrong because it was never released.

The question the Electronium was asking — what does it mean for a machine to compose, rather than to execute — has not been answered. It is still open. It is one of the questions Kaiku is sitting next to, not answering.

*See also:* Scott, Raymond. Raymond Scott Quintette recordings (1937–1939). The compositional grammar predates the electronic work. The structures are mechanical, almost algorithmic beneath the surface. The Looney Tunes licensing is incidental to the source.

---

## sonic lineage

**Cellar Darling.** *This Is the Sound* (2017) and subsequent work.

Anna Murphy's hurdy-gurdy is the closest existing model for what Tyhjyydenkaiku approximates synthetically. The wheel drone, the voice above it, the rosin texture — present in a contemporary rock context without becoming folk pastiche. Cellar Darling showed that the hurdy-gurdy register can carry full harmonic and dynamic weight. Tyhjyydenkaiku is what happens when you try to build that inside an FM synthesizer.

---

**Moonsorrow.** Finnish black metal, 1995–present.

Ritual register. Long form. Finnish. The music is treated as ceremony, not performance. Moonsorrow draws from the same well this project draws from — and draws from it as ritual. The silence has a name there too.

The long-form composition ethic (the Jumalhämärä lineage: the 13-minute form is the form) is in this lineage. So is the cosmological weight — the sense that the music is not describing something large but is the large thing, at reduced scale.

## standards and normative references

**Discordian Standards Body (DSB).** *Principia Discordia: Or How I Found Goddess and What I Did to Her When I Found Her — Temporal Reference Framework, Fifth Edition.* Hill, Malaclypse the Younger (rapporteur); Ravenhurst, Omar Khayyam (co-rapporteur). 3125 YOLD / 1959 CE (initial draft); ratified 3129 YOLD / 1963 CE; current edition 3136 YOLD / 1970 CE.

Normative reference for the five-season, 73-day-per-season temporal framework implemented in `kuule.html` and `birch.html`. Seasons: Chaos, Discord, Confusion, Bureaucracy, The Aftermath. Intercalary provision: St. Tib's Day, inserted between Discord 59 and Discord 60 in years divisible by four, except centuries, except quadricenturies. Days of the week: Sweetmorn, Boomtime, Pungenday, Prickle-Prickle, Setting Orange.

*Copyright status: unresolved.* The normative text carries the notice "All Rites Reversed — reprint what you like," which the DSB appears to intend as a permissive grant. Whether this constitutes a valid public domain dedication, an informal licence, or an enforceable instrument under the applicable jurisdiction (Discordia, a non-signatory to the Berne Convention) has not been adjudicated. Users of this temporal framework are advised to proceed on the assumption that the DSB may at any time clarify its position. The DSB has not clarified its position. The DSB is unlikely to clarify its position. Implement accordingly.

## not adopted

**Cage, John.** *4'33"* (1952).

The performer sits at the instrument. Nothing is played. Four minutes and thirty-three seconds. The ambient sounds of the room become the music. Cage's argument: there is no silence; unintended sound fills any space; the composition is the act of attending to what was already there.

This is not what the thirteen-minute compositions are doing. The drone does not stop. Hiljaisuus is not the absence of sound that reveals the room. The silence in this universe has content — Station Nine is a place, not a frame. Sitting with these compositions for thirteen minutes is not Cage's practice and should not be described as such.

The formal influence is acknowledged. The philosophical framework is declined.

---

**Theremin, Léon.** The theremin (1920).

The capacitive proximity interface — pitch and volume controlled by the position of the player's hands in space, no contact required — is one of the most expressive performance technologies ever built. It could be applied here. It is not.

Kaiku is an FM synthesizer. Its expression lives in the FM parameter space, in velocity, in the operator topology. The theremin's paradigm is gestural control of a continuous-wave oscillator. That is a different instrument solving a different problem. The technology is acknowledged. The decision is deliberate.

## theoretical reference

**Forte, Allen.** *The Structure of Atonal Music.* Yale University Press, 1973.

The set notation — prime forms, interval classes, pitch-class sets — is precise and useful. It is used here where useful.

The analytical framework it was built to serve is not this project's analytical framework.

*The Structure of Atonal Music* was written for music without tonal centers: music where the question "what key is this in?" has no answer, and where pitch-class relationships must carry the structural weight that tonal function used to carry. Forte's apparatus is exactly right for that music. It asks the right questions of it.

This project's music has a tonal center. F# Phrygian. The characteristic semitone is G natural — the b2, the Phrygian blade, the pull toward the root. Every scale degree has a function. The 8-cent detuning is a timbre decision, not a pitch-class decision. The question "what does this note do?" has an answer in the key.

Asking Forte's questions of this music would be asking the wrong questions carefully. The set notation survives that criticism. The theory does not apply here.

Set notation without set theory is a tool without its theoretical obligations. That is exactly what is wanted.
