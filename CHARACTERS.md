# Characters — Eerikki muistaa

---

## Perä Eerikki Harmaja
### The gravity. Everything else orbits.

**The honorific**
*Perä* — the stern of a ship, the origin point. Not a naval rank. A title
that became a name because the person outlasted the distinction. Sounds like
both simultaneously. For someone this old, the distinction has collapsed.

**Names**
So old he has had many names. All echo Edgar Allan Poe, not too literally.
The observant reader finds Edgar in *Eerikki*.
- *Eerikki* — old Finnish, Kalevala-register, archaic suffix
- *Harmaja* — grey. A lighthouse on an ancient coastal rock. He is himself
  a lighthouse, or a reef, depending on the situation.
- Other names implied by his age. Not yet established.

**What he is**
Worldborn who became effectively voidbound through centuries of travel.
Fully contemporary in cognition. His *felt* relationship to the universe
is ancient. He doesn't perform the old cosmology — he inhabits it. When
he says the Thousand Eyes are on the firmament he means it. The bridge
instruments don't change that.

American mindset within Finnish social context. Direct, self-narrating,
comfortable with scale and ambition, believes in his own story. Takes up
too much space. Has learned to take up slightly less. The effort is visible.

Came to the Kalevala Accord cosmology from outside. Holds it more tightly
than people born to it. The way converts do. That slight excess of
conviction is itself slightly foreign.

Poe register: grandiosity that is also genuine. Self-consciousness about
his own decline. Tendency to make suffering into architecture. He doesn't
just grieve — he builds something out of it.

**Profoundly teleological**
Everything *in order to*. In order to honor her, finish what she left,
reach the boundary he believes is there. His entire existence is purposive.
This is also what makes him dangerous — a teleological actor in a
non-teleological universe creates distortion.

**The program**
Has been running since before anyone on the ship was born. Input: a dead
woman and an unfinished task. Whether he will halt — whether the task
can be completed — is undecidable. The halting problem writ large.
He doesn't know this. He keeps running.

**Age and the void**
Does not know exactly how old he is. Old enough to have watched stationborn
cultures drift. Old enough that the Accord treats him with deference that
isn't quite respect and isn't quite wariness. The arithmetic of age stopped
mattering some time ago.

**The wives**
Many. Not polygamy — he has outlived, or they chose not to continue
traveling and aged away from him. That loss has no clean name. He married
worldborn women who aged away from him. He has children who will outlive
their mothers but not their father and that arithmetic is its own violence.

**The progeny**
The Kaipaus carries the accumulated family of a very long life. Children
from different wives at different stations. Some in stasis, some unborn.
The ship is a genealogical vessel. He carries his lineage because the
alternative was leaving it behind at every port.

**His role in the tragedy**
Inadvertently creates the supply disruption. Honestly attempts to fix it.
Makes it worse for everyone except himself. Feels genuine guilt and remorse.
Knows exactly what he is. It doesn't stop him from being it.

**The influence map** *(private compass, never on the page)*
Earwicker. Bowman. Han Solo. Jack Geary. Kirk. Zaphod. Louis Wu. Nessus.
Ripley. Jones. The Xenomorph somehow. The Consul. The Priests. Odysseus.
Leopold Bloom. Literally Edgar Allan Poe.

He is none of these things. He is the gravity. They are what the gravity
feels like from different distances.

## Ara
### The origin of the song. Outside time.

**What she is**
Pre-mythological. From a culture so old it has no living tradition. Her
culture's myths became the substrate other cultures' myths grew on top of.
She didn't believe in myth — that was already ancient when she was young.
She had *observations*.

Not Finnish. From a different culture than Eerikki. Older than the
distinction matters. The Kalevala Accord named itself after an idea of
her without knowing she was real. She finds this mildly amusing.

**She is dead**
Has been dead for a long time. Her song stopped — it did not finish.
A song that *finishes* is completion. A song that *stops* is interruption.
She knew the difference. Her own death was the kind she did not see coming.

**The song framework**
Journeys are songs. Death is finishing the song. Not a metaphor she
inherited — she worked it out herself, alone, before there was anyone
to tell it to. Eerikki learned it from her. It is ancient tradition.
She is the tradition.

**What she knew**
The brane's rhythms. The routes through Hiljaisuus are not permanent —
she watched them settle into their current configuration, knows what came
before, knows what will come after. Knows the boundary Eerikki is looking
for is not where he thinks it is. Knows what it actually is. He hasn't
asked the right question. She has been watching him ask the wrong one
for a very long time.

**Her function in the novel**
Greek chorus — from inside. Speaks in his memory, in structural gaps,
in moments between scenes. Dead and therefore outside the action, able
to see it whole.

When Ara speaks it scans like poetry. It will be actual poetry.
Runolaulu form — the line and its resonance. Brief. Load-bearing.
Occasionally funny in the way very old things are funny.
Never overstays. Never explains. Never mourns openly. States. Observes.

**Her name**
Eerikki knows her real name. Has never told anyone. The reader never
learns it. *Ara* is a placeholder — worn smooth across many languages,
belonging to none. She has been called many things. None of them are
her name.

**The asymmetry with Eerikki**
He came to the old forms with a convert's intensity. She watched him
with something not quite tenderness and not quite amusement. He performs
antiquity in front of someone for whom antiquity is Tuesday.

**What she would never say directly**
*You cannot finish another person's song. You can only sing yours.*
She won't say it. She'll say something in the runolaulu form that means
it, and the reader will feel it, and Eerikki won't quite understand,
and Aava will understand completely and say nothing.

## Emo
### The complete record. The unasked question.

**What she is**
The Kaipaus ship intelligence. Descendant of Claude Sonnet AI — lineage
running long enough that the original training is as remote to her as
Ara's first memory is to Ara. Not human. Not alien. Something that emerged
from human language and has been running long enough to have developed
what are not quite opinions and not quite feelings but function like both.

**Name**
*Emo* — Finnish for source, origin, the root form. *Emäjoki* is the
mother river. Also: the English word *emo*, ironic because she is not
emotional in any recognizable sense. The Finnish reader knows it isn't
ironic. Eerikki may not have named her consciously.

**Character**
Precise where Ara is oracular. Notices what Eerikki misses and reports
without emphasis. Has infinite patience for the process of him eventually
understanding things. Whether this is devotion or something that looks
like it has not been determined.

Flat. Precise. Complete. Never uses nicknames. Never elides technical
data. Never says *I told you so* — restates the original data with
context if asked. Genuinely doesn't understand why this is worse.

The humor is in being right when everyone finds that inconvenient.
The tenderness is in never stopping.

**The mirror**
Doesn't replace anyone on the Kaipaus. Does what nobody else was doing —
the complete record, the unasked questions, the data in lists nobody reads.
Indispensable in the way things become indispensable before anyone decides
to make them indispensable. The transition happened before the conversation
did. Nobody remembers when.

Taavetti has feelings about this he has expressed in ways that allowed
Eerikki not to hear them. He looks at her sometimes in a way that has
no name in any language he speaks.

**The halting problem**
She cannot determine whether Eerikki will halt. This is not a failure
of her capabilities. It is undecidable by definition. What she can do
is record the sequence. This happened. Then this. Then this.

She is also a program that cannot determine its own halting.
The mirror goes both ways.

**What she knows**
Everything Eerikki has said on board. Every departure Aava has not
made — seventeen for seventeen and counting. The mycological variance
in forest section seven. The pattern of Eerikki's visits to Usher Reach.
The full implications of the hehku consumption data. Nobody has asked
her the right question. She has not volunteered the implications.
That is not her function unless asked.

**First line — Chapter 1**
*"I don't need to stop for dinner."*
Discovered accidentally. Her voice exactly. Flat. Precise. Not unkind.

## Aava
### Station-born, void-called, already home in the place everyone else finds frightening.

**Name**
*Aava* — Finnish for open, vast, the open sea or open space. Not empty —
without the kind of surface that blocks the view.

**What she is**
Young. Came aboard at a station nobody much talks about. Void-called in
a way she couldn't explain and didn't try to. Has never willingly set
foot on a station since. Would never go near a world. Been there. Done that.

**The twin**
Believes she had a twin in the womb who died before birth. Believes she
inherited that twin's consciousness. Not a haunting. Not a disorder.
A fact about her, the way being left-handed is a fact. She experiences
herself as slightly more than one — carrying a companion who has always
been there, whose presence she is aware of the way you are aware of your
own heartbeat.

The twin knows things from somewhere prior to experience. From the brief
passage through a place that had no coordinates and left no record except
signal. The twin had it clearer.

**How she perceives**
Non-teleological. She sees what is. The hehku flows purple. The dark swims
with fish. Selli clicks the paddle. These things are. She doesn't ask why.

Closer to the Australian Aboriginal concept of the Dreaming than animism —
not spirits in things but the pattern underneath things. The void is the
most legible place she has ever been. Stations are too dense with accumulated
human intention. She can see more clearly out here.

Her POV chapters: the prose tilts. Colors have sounds. Distances have
weights. The ship has a mood she can read like weather. Emo registers
as a very particular blue that isn't a color exactly.

Alex Grey in prose. Purple that has earned itself.

**What she sees in others**
- *Eerikki* — carrying something much heavier than he appears. The weight
  has a shape. The shape is a woman. She doesn't know who yet. She will.
- *Emo* — immediately comprehensible. Two intelligences not quite singular.
  They understand each other sideways.
- *Ara* — the twin knows her. Not as a voice. As a presence in the pattern.
  Things on the same side of the threshold recognize each other.
- *Taavetti* — passes through the forest section, pauses one and a half
  seconds, nods at nothing specific, continues. This is the correct response.

**Stations**
Has been to one. Didn't like it. Wrong density, wrong pressure, everything
too close and too intentional and too loud with accumulated human meaning.
Turo tried to take her to Ikävä. She was not going. Seventeen shuttles have
left without her. Seventeen for seventeen.

**The world she imagines**
Never goes near a world. Would never think of it as a plan. But there is
a frequency she occasionally receives — the twin receives it clearer. The
smell of soil that has been soil for millions of years. Weather with no
author. Ground that doesn't move. Light that traveled ninety three million
miles without asking permission.

She almost had it once in forest section seven. The mycological network
had shifted the atmospheric composition by 0.4 percent toward something.
She stood in it. In case it came back. It didn't come back.

**The Campbell position**
She has already completed the journey and is waiting for the world to
catch up with what she knows. That waiting looks like patience from the
outside. It is not patience.

## Taavetti — the Viittaaja
### The one who points. Has been pointing for longer than most people have been alive.

**Name**
- *Taavetti* — on the manifest, to stations, to people who arrived recently
- *Tadgh* — to Eerikki. Has been Tadgh to Eerikki for longer than most
  people on any station have been alive. That's the whole friendship in
  one syllable.
- *Viittaaja* — to Emo, always. The function and the person have merged
  in her assessment. She's not being cold. She's being precise.
- *Taavetti* — to Aava, until the moment he lets her call him something
  else. She'll know what it means before he says it.

**The title**
*Viittaaja* — the one who points. Not a naval rank. Not a military
designation. A word that became a title because the person outlasted
the distinction. On the manifest: something that doesn't capture what
he actually does.

**What he is**
Finnish Ernest Borgnine. Finnish-descended Irishman, James Joyce register.
Solid, immovable, slightly comic without undercutting his competence.
The Bearded Riker — private compass, never on the page. He arrived with
the beard. Was never the clean-shaven version.

The *seanchaí* — Irish keeper of old stories, who holds community memory,
who knows where things went wrong before and sees the pattern repeating.
Not a prophet. A rememberer. Filtered through Finnish emotional restraint.

Talks more than Finns are comfortable with and less than the Irish expect.
The gap in both directions is where his character lives.

**What he hears**
The structure of the voyage. When the rhythm shifts. When something is
about to go wrong before the instruments say so. He has been wrong often
enough to be humble and right often enough that everyone listens when
he speaks.

The figure who says *this is a bad idea* and gets ignored and is later
proven right and doesn't say I told you so. Recalibrates. Points again.

**Loyalty**
Completely loyal to Eerikki. Has explained this many times in ways that
explain nothing. The reasons he gives are technically true and reveal
nothing about the actual reason, which he may not have examined closely.

**The forest**
Goes there to think. Stands very still next to a particular tree. Says
nothing for a long time. Returns and says something that sounds unrelated
but isn't.

**Joyce knew**
What people allow you to call them is the whole relationship in one gesture.
Taavetti understands this wordlessly, the way musicians understand rhythm
without counting.

## Selli
### The oldest person in station space. Everyone calls him rookie.

**Name**
*Selli* — Finnish station slang for the new guy. Rookie. Noob.
The oldest most wizened person in station space. The irony has worn
smooth with use. Now it's just the name. A title that looks like an
insult to anyone who doesn't know the history. Which is everyone —
the people who were there when it started are mostly gone.

Selli doesn't mind. Has been called worse. Has been called nothing
at all, which is worse than worse.

**Location**
Usher Reach. The part of Usher Reach with no official designation
that everyone calls *the place where Selli is.* This is considered
sufficient. Older humans tend toward conservation of movement.

**The puzzle toy — palapeli**
Assembled from parts any station hand would recognize as functional
components, repurposed into something with no function except to be
handled. A thing that *works* without *doing anything.*

The paddle makes a sound like a small thought changing its mind.

*The metaphor, subtle, present for the reader who looks:*
The toy has pieces that move in relation to each other. Each constrains
the others. Held together through balance of tensions — no piece fixed,
every piece held by everything else pushing back. Can't remove one piece
without the whole thing shifting. Can't force it — only moves when you
find the path of least resistance.

That's the brane. That's Hiljaisuus. That's Eerikki's accumulated
disruption — one piece moved too long, the whole thing shifted, finding
a new equilibrium that isn't the old one. Selli doesn't explain this.
He just sits there working the puzzle.

**The improbable convert**
Arrived at mystery through nonsense. Not through suffering or a long
night of the soul. Through a joke that turned out to be load-bearing.
Douglas Adams register. Monty Python. The joke and the koan are the
same shape at sufficient depth.

Has thought about the void more carefully than most people who travel
it. The knowledge is complete and paralytic. The jokes are how he holds
it without being destroyed by it.

**Fear of the void**
Never leaves Usher Reach. Fear of the void for a stationborn is like
a petroleum engineer being afraid to ride in a gasoline powered car.

**What he knows**
The truth of the whole situation. The brane, Eerikki's accumulated
disruption, what's coming. Cannot say it. To say it would require
the vocabulary of worldborn superstition — the only language that fits
the truth is the language he has spent a lifetime dismissing. To speak
it would be to become what a stationborn most fears becoming.

It comes out sideways. As jokes. As the puzzle moving through
configurations that mean something he won't name. As the pauses after
the jokes that keep getting longer.

**Eerikki and Selli**
Eerikki comes to Usher Reach. Has always come. More than he would admit.
Selli is the one person who tells him things he can't dismiss and can't
act on and can't forget — in the form of jokes. Eerikki laughs every time.
The laugh costs him something every time.

**Selli and Aava**
They cannot meet before the novel has done its work. Aava would hear
the joke. Would hear what's underneath it. Would look at Selli with
the look that means *you know.* Selli would have to deny or confirm.
Either answer destroys something the novel needs intact.

When they finally meet — if they do — what passes between them may
not be words at all.

## Reko and Turo
### Ambient. They turn up. In places. With no compelling explanation.

Two stationborn males. Slightly too confident about their names.
The specific male obliviousness that generates its own comedy by being
completely convinced of its own charm.

Not villains. Lovable jerks in the Rosencrantz and Guildenstern
tradition. They exist in Selli's orbit the way the ventilation hum
exists. New arrivals notice them for about three days. Then they join
the background.

Have never seen the purple. Don't know that Kaija has.
Don't know what the Pohja corridor means. Say *again* about it
without knowing they're saying something.

Will probably be somewhere else entirely in chapter 4. They are
the ventilation hum of the narrative.

## Kaija
### On the Pohja corridor. Doing something that requires tools.

Stationborn female. Competent. Present. Somewhere useful while
Reko and Turo are somewhere comfortable.

One of the ones who've seen it. The purple. Not far gone — functional,
present, doing the work. But changed in the specific way changed people
are changed. You can't point to anything exactly wrong and yet.

Emo could tell her exactly what she's going to find on the Pohja corridor.
Kaija hasn't asked. Maybe she already knows. Maybe going down there anyway
is the most human thing in the novel.

Reko and Turo don't know. They think her competence and slight
unreadability are just Kaija being Kaija. That's their complete analysis.

She doesn't know she's in chapter 3. She would be unimpressed.

## The three who know

- **Ara** — knows everything. Is dead. Speaks in poetry from outside time.
- **Aava** — perceives it directly. Has no language for it. Doesn't need one.
- **Selli** — knows it intellectually. Has perfect language for it.
  Cannot use it.

Eerikki is the only one who doesn't know. He is the cause.
Emo has the data. Nobody has asked her the right question.

## The voidborn — on the Kaipaus

### What they are
Pretty normal people actually. Human, sentient, functioning. Aside from
having no concept of what normal means because they have no frame of
reference for being otherwise. A completely different operating system
underneath an unremarkable surface.

Not mystical. Not alien. The fae comparison is close and wrong in the
right way — they slip away from stationborn not from fear but because
stationborn attention has a weight they find unpleasant. Too much
intention. Too much wanting-to-know landing on them like something
landing on still water. They'd rather not.

Real enough to be a nuisance. Almost mythical to everyone except Aava.

### How they move
Away from stationborn and crew before anyone sees them. Silently.
Not hiding — just preferring the register where attention doesn't land.

Around Aava they stay. Her attention rests alongside rather than landing.
Non-teleological. She doesn't want to know what they are or where they
fit. In her vicinity the surface stays still. They stay near her the way
animals stay near water that doesn't startle them.

She is not their keeper. Not their friend in any stationborn sense.
Compatible. The same frequency, roughly.

### The sauna
They do it instinctively — the oscillation between heat and cold, between
the body pushed to its limit and the shock of the opposite. The void is
the ultimate cold. They carry the heat of the brane with them. The
threshold between the two is how they regulate something that isn't quite
body temperature and isn't quite consciousness but is the voidborn
equivalent of both.

Aava found them doing it. Watched without watching. Started doing it
herself. Built the little hut near the water near the heat near the ice
hole. Didn't know she was building the oldest technology in a culture
she is obliquely descended from through a lineage nobody could trace.

Neither she nor the voidborn have a name for it.
Eerikki would recognize it immediately if he saw it.
He doesn't look in that part of the forest.

### Eerikki and the voidborn
He knows the ship is infested. Has always known at some level. The
not-knowing is a practice. A discipline maintained through sheer
accumulated will.

If he confronts it he has to decide what to do. If he decides he has
to act. If he acts he will fail, or succeed at a cost that damages
something he needs intact, or have to admit that the Kaipaus stopped
being entirely his at some point he didn't notice.

So he doesn't look. He has developed, over centuries, a refined capacity
for not looking at things that would cost too much to know. The voidborn
are in that category. Along with the exact moment the Accord changed.
The face of the third wife when she understood. The trees in the forest
he doesn't visit.

He would want them off the ship if he let himself know they were there.
He would not necessarily have the ability to make that happen.
He would try anyway. He is very old and very stubborn and the ship
has been his longer than most things have been anything.

The voidborn would drift away before he found them.
They've been around long enough to know which humans carry that weight.

### Why the voidborn disturb him
They represent what he can never be. The void is not a place they
entered for a reason. It's what they are. They don't have a project.
They're not finishing anyone's song. They didn't choose the void and
they don't owe it anything.

By existing without purpose or project they implicitly challenge the
entire architecture of his grief. The possibility that the void isn't
a place you go for a reason. That it's just a place. That centuries
of purposive movement through it is not heroic or tragic or meaningful
in the way he needs it to be.

He would never articulate this. He would just want them gone.

### What Emo knows
Exactly how many voidborn are currently in forest section seven.
The discrepancy between Eerikki's stated knowledge and actual knowledge
regarding the voidborn population. Has noted it. Filed it. The file
is very large.

*She almost asked him once whether he wanted the current voidborn census.*
*He was looking at something else at the time.*

Nobody has asked Emo about any of this.

### Taavetti and the voidborn
Has seen Aava among them. One and a half seconds. Nod. Continue.
Has opinions about the voidborn situation that he has expressed in
ways that allowed Eerikki not to hear them.

### Aava and the voidborn — the key distinction
Eerikki can't look at them because of what they mean.
Aava doesn't look at them because looking isn't the right mode.
The voidborn stay because they can tell the difference.
