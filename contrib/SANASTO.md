# sanasto

*A small glossary. Finnish is structural to this project, not decorative. You don't have to speak it. You do have to respect that it's doing real work.*

---

## counting

You don't have to count in Finnish. birch counts your moves for you in Finnish, and the game works fine whether you know what *kolme* means or not.

But if you do know — if *kolme* lands when you've just made your third move, and something clicks — that's the good version. The language is there for people who want to meet it. It won't chase you if you don't.

The numbers you'll see: *yksi kaksi kolme neljä viisi kuusi seitsemän kahdeksan yhdeksän kymmenen.* One through ten. After that it gets compositional and interesting, but the game probably won't go that well for you anyway.

## kaiku

*echo; reverb*

The synthesizer's name. First impression on KVR: delay plugin. Known issue, not a reason to rename. Front-load "FM synthesizer" in any listing or post. The name is correct — it just needs a little help.

*(Native speaker note from Mystran, Helsinki: `kaikun` as a proper name vs. `kaiun` as a Finnish common word. Both registers held simultaneously. This is intentional.)*

---

## hiljaisuus

*silence*

The pulse at 0.15Hz. The ninth station. Not a metaphor — a place. In the interface, violet means you're near it. Violet appears when something is at a threshold. Not decoratively.

---

## tyhjyydenkaiku

*echo of the void*

The primary patch. Three FM stacks approximating a hurdy gurdy wheel and a human mouth. The 8-cent detuning is the wheel. Don't tune it out.

---

## koivuhuilu

*birch flute*

Warm, breathy, slightly flat. The physical instrument it's modelling was slightly flat. The model inherits this. It's correct.

---

## kuolinvihellys

*death whistle*

A threshold instrument. It has been put down. It has not been played since. The reason given for putting it down was wrong.

---

## heksoja

*hexagons (plural)*

Correct plural of *heksa*. Use this, not `heksejä`. (Mystran again. He's right.)

---

## on the grilli

*version marker*

The grilli menu in the README is the version. What's smoking is shipping. What's in the fire isn't on the menu yet.
