# on reading this project

Start anywhere.

The games are the fastest way in. Play kuule for three minutes. Look at birch. You will know more about what this project is than any document can tell you. The documents are there when you want them. They are not a prerequisite.

---

## what is load-bearing

Three things you need before you touch code or copy:

**988.** Know it. Hold it with the gravity it deserves. The full note is in `CONDUCT.md`.

**The style guide.** `STYLE.md`. Not optional for anything that ships. Optional for everything else.

**The sanasto.** `SANASTO.md`. Finnish is structural. You do not have to speak it. You have to not override it.

That is the load-bearing set. Everything else is depth. Read it when you want depth.

---

## what is ambient

The essays at rebraining.org. `arch/time.md`. The composition notes in `comp/`. The percussion briefs. The IR provenance. The mission document. All of it is real, all of it belongs here, none of it is required before you can contribute something useful.

If you find yourself three hours into a rabbit hole and you've lost the thread of what you were originally doing — that is fine. The rabbit hole was probably worth it. The thread will be here when you come back. Nothing shifts so dramatically between visits that you can't find your footing.

---

## on returning

The project does not track your absence. There is no correct re-entry. You can come back after a week or a year and pick up wherever makes sense. You do not need to apologize for the gap or explain the interval or catch up on everything before you are allowed to contribute again.

If something has changed while you were gone, the diff will tell you. If something you built is no longer compatible, the PR will say so. Neither of these is a judgment.

---

## on reading in the wrong order

There is no wrong order. The project has structure but not a required sequence. The person who starts with the SANASTO and the person who starts with the FM spec and the person who starts with kuule and never reads anything else — all of these are valid.

The work does not require you to have read all of it to engage with any of it.

---

## on losing interest mid-document

Also fine. The documents are not linear by necessity. If a section isn't landing, skip it. If you come back to it later and it lands differently, good. If you never come back to it, the project will survive.

---

The pulse runs at 0.15Hz. Six point six seven seconds. Everything here breathes at that rate or slower. There is no urgency that the work itself has not put there.
