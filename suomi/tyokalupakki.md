# työkalupakki — the toolbox

*Dick Darby puts his tools away. In Finnish.*

---

## tools that turn — angles

**kulma** — angle, corner. The basic unit of where two things meet.
**suorakulma** — right angle. *suora* (straight) + *kulma*. The angle that doesn't apologize.
**terävä kulma** — acute angle. *terävä* — sharp. The angle that cuts.
**tylppä kulma** — obtuse angle. *tylppä* — blunt, dull. The angle that doesn't.
**kierre** — spiral, thread, twist. The screw's soul. Also: the thing that won't let go.
**kierros** — rotation, revolution, one full turn. Also: a round of drinks.
**kääntyä** — to turn (verb). The act, not the result.

---

## tools that measure — numbers

**nolla** — zero. Nothing. Also: a useless person.
**yksi** — one.
**kaksi** — two.
**kolme** — three.
**neljä** — four.
**viisi** — five.
**kuusi** — six. Also: spruce tree. Finnish didn't see a conflict.
**seitsemän** — seven.
**kahdeksan** — eight.
**yhdeksän** — nine.
**kymmenen** — ten.

**mitta** — measure, dimension. What the tool reads.
**vesivaaka** — level. Literally: water balance. The instrument that tells you the truth about flat.

## tools that hit

*Not Cannibal Corpse. Katatonia. Alcest. The weight without the gore.*
*The Finnish language already knows how to do this.*

**vasara** — hammer. The word lands like the thing. No decoration required.

**isku** — blow, strike, impact. Single syllable. The moment the thing connects.

**murtaa** — to break, to fracture. Also: to overcome. One word for both.
The cobbler knows which he means. So does the station.

**särkeä** — to shatter. Also: to ache. The same word.
Finnish didn't separate these. The language was right.

**musertaa** — to crush, to overwhelm. The weight that doesn't lift.

**hakata** — to hack, to beat, to chop. Repeated blows. Methodical.

**repiä** — to tear. The slow kind.

**repäistä** — to rip. The sudden kind.

**tuhota** — to destroy. Final. No ambiguity.

**kaataa** — to fell. What you do to a tree. What the tree experiences.

## tonal register — for future development

Alcest (French blackgaze): beauty as weight. The gorgeous thing that crushes you
because it's beautiful. Light through water through ice. The station has places that do this.

Katatonia (Swedish): grief as texture. Not melodrama — the low persistent frequency
of loss that becomes the room's ambient condition. Everyone in the room has adjusted
to it. No one mentions it. Reko stopped hearing it on day three.

Insomnium (Finnish): already in the sanasto. *tumma.* The native instance.

The novel's register lives here. Not in the violence — in the weight.
