# sanasto — vocabulary

*Finnish for people who don't speak Navajo.*

---

## the novel's finnish

**eerikki muistaa** — Eerikki remembers. Lowercase intentional.
**hehku** — glow, radiance, heat burning from within. Never "fuel."
**hiljaisuus** — the Silence. The chokepoint.
**kaipaus** — longing. The ship's name. Named for a person without being named for her.
**kaamos** — the polar night. The darkness before the light returns.
**emo** — source, origin, root form. Ironic English emo because she is not emotional.
**aava** — open, vast. Not empty — without the surface that blocks the view.
**taavetti** — a name. Tadgh to Eerikki. The whole friendship in one syllable.
**selli** — station slang for new guy/rookie. The oldest person in the diaspora.
**hiljaisuus** — silence, the void chokepoint.
**palapeli** — puzzle. Selli's toy.
**viittaaja** — pointer, indicator. Emo's name for Taavetti. Function and person merged.
**väylä** — channel, passage, fairway. The threshold pilot. The navigable route.
**pohja** — bottom, foundation, deep north. The corridor Kaija keeps going back to.
**tietäjä** — the one who knows. Power through knowing the origin of things.
**joik** — Sámi invocation. Not a song about something. The song is the thing.
**runolaulu** — Finnish rune-song. The line and its resonance.
**tumma** — dark. Eino Leino. Insomnium source.
**muistaa** — to remember. Present tense. He remembers. It is happening now.

## sleep and dream

**uni** — sleep. Also dream. The same word. The boundary doesn't exist.
**unelma** — the aspirational dream. The waking vision.
**haave** — reverie, longing, the daydream that doesn't quite become a plan.
**näky** — vision, apparition, something seen.
**painajainen** — nightmare. Literally: the presser. Something pressing down in sleep.
**nukkua** — to sleep (verb).
**nukahtaa** — to fall asleep.

## the gap

**myötähäpeä** — secondhand embarrassment, but larger. Acutely experiencing
another's emotional exposure. The gap between what someone is feeling
and what language can hold, inhabited by a third party.

**väli** — interval, gap, the space between.

**myota** — stripped transliteration. The space between what you're carrying
and what I can reach from here.

## tools and projects

**taju** — the moment of comprehension landing. *Tajuatko?* — do you get it.
The name for the Scrivener MCP integration. The tool perceives the shape.
The name is the act.

**solmu** — knot, node. The connection point. Also nautical speed.

## streetwise

**eläköön Suomi** — Long live Finland. Optative of *elää*, to live. Also used at birthdays.

**perkele** — the Devil. Finland's signature expletive. Guttural, final. Other languages have equivalents. None sound like this.

**siisti** — sick, cool, clean. Compliment and aesthetic standard simultaneously.

## local color

**grilli** — the grill kiosk. Working class. After bars close, everyone ends up there.
The transaction is a sausage. The actual business is everything else.

**nakkiluukku** — the sausage hatch. A small window in a wall. The person on the other
side knows things.

**Selli's grilli** — the alliteration is a joik. The hatch is the point.

**nakkiluukku** in stationspace — every language on the station has a word for stink.
One of those words is also Selli's Sausage Hatch. The Finnish compound is the
faint ur-echo heard in the name that stuck. The name belongs to the place, not the man.

## notes on finnish

The language doesn't borrow well. It forces its own structure onto imported
concepts. Things that pass through Finnish come out changed in ways that are
hard to reverse-engineer. This is why it works as a filter.

Losing the language means losing the knowledge. The morphology carries the
cosmology. Pearse knew this about Irish. It applies here.

Finnish and Navajo are the same in this dimension. Neither borrows well.
Both change what passes through them. The cosmology is in the grammar.
This is why the suomi directory exists — not for translation, for resistance.
