# läpikatse

Through-gaze. The gaze that sees through the frame to what's underneath.

The mode of perception activated when you know the parking lot and you read
the romanticization and you see the gap and you know the author sees the gap
and you watch them choose the frame anyway.

The choice is the betrayal. The knowledge that they know better is what
turns disappointment into something harder.

Not the rage itself. The perceptual mode that produces the rage when the
author fails it.

---

The Basha's parking lot. The drum circle. The speed bump that takes out
your leaf springs. The dogs. The literature doesn't hold these simultaneously.
It chooses. Even the writers who were born there — especially them,
because the outside audience creates the pressure to choose.

The voidborn have läpikatse as their default state. They don't acquire it.
They were never without it. They carry these dimensions without carrying them
because they have no outside audience to perform for.

The stationborn find their attention disturbing. It doesn't accept the frame.

---

## the gap this word lives in

Between Tony Hillerman's Monument Valley and the actual speed bump on the
road into Kayenta.

Between the ceremony and the parking lot.

Between what the story is supposed to be about and what it's actually like.

Between the literature and the place's relationship to itself.

The worldborn in this novel live in that gap. They don't appear as characters.
Their weight is in the white space.

The rounding error.
