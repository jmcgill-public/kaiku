## What this is

**Kaamos** is simultaneously:

1. A **novel** — *Eerikki Muistaa* (Finnish: Edgar Remembers). Literary science fiction. The protagonist caused a cosmological disruption, doesn't fully understand what he did, and is genuinely trying to fix it while making it worse for everyone except himself.

2. A **video game** — a glyph-generating puzzle game that teaches the player the novel's language and cosmology without announcing that it's doing so. The player learns to read the world before they read about it.

3. A **music project** — Tyhjyydenkaiku (Echo of the Void), a ~4:30 atmospheric piece for KVR Music Cafe. F# Phrygian. Finnish mouth music, FM synthesis, a birch flute, a death whistle. The cue sheet is in kaiku_cue_sheet.md.

4. An **ur-myth** — a cosmological framework that aligns with the author's actual beliefs about the structure of the universe. The Vedic Shesha parallel (Shesha = "that which remains" = *muistaa* = Edgar remembers) is load-bearing, not decorative.

These are not separate projects that share a name. They are the same project in four registers.

## The structural law

**Hegelian Triadic Epiphany / Dual Correctness:**

Two truths collide. Both are valid. Neither resolves. No synthesis. The collision IS the meaning.

The novel does not explain this. It enacts it. Every character who knows something knows it incompletely. Every character who acts, acts on incomplete knowledge and correct instinct simultaneously. The reader holds both.

This is not a theme. It is the load-bearing structure. Everything else hangs from it.

**The load-bearing image** (from the inciting sequence):
The last moment before a hanging. A bird with a broken wing, singing on the branch the rope is tied to. The string laments what the bird cannot. Neither one is wrong. Both are real. This is Dual Correctness in its first and most complete expression.

## Cosmology

The universe consists of branes separated by the void. The void has no spacetime but it has structure. The structure is navigable. The navigation is the game.

**Hehku** — the energy currency of civilization. Flows between stations via the void. Routed by stationhands who do not understand what they are routing or what it costs when they fail.

**The Expanse** — the inhabited region. Eight named stations arranged on a radius-2 hex grid. One additional station that is not on any map.

**Stations** — hubs of civilization, infrastructure, population. Worldborn live on worlds (planets). Stationborn are native to the void-adjacent architecture. This distinction is political, economic, and cosmological simultaneously.

**Station 9 — Hiljaisuus** — The Silence. The unmapped station. Not a place you go. A fold in the brane topology. It is always present. It is the center of the glyph ring in the logo. The hiljaisuus-pulse animation runs at exactly 0.15Hz (period 6.67s). This is hardcoded. Do not change it.

**Shesha connection:** In Vedic cosmology, Shesha is the cosmic serpent who remains when all else is destroyed — the substrate of creation, the remainder. Muistaa in Finnish means "to remember." Edgar (Eerikki) remembers. Shesha remains. These are the same word in different registers. The shesha.stl sculpture (9 cobra heads, tapered coiled body) encodes this physically.

## Characters — canonical register

### Eerikki (Edgar)
The center. Not the hero — the condition everything else happens around. Ancient. Caused the disruption. Genuinely trying to fix it. Making it worse for everyone except himself. His ship has a forest and an ocean because he couldn't stop being from somewhere that had those things. The grief is real. The engineering is extraordinary. Both are true simultaneously. **Void archetype: Bound.**

### Taavetti (Taav)
Eerikki's oldest friend. Has been pointing at the problem longer than most people have been alive. Eerikki hasn't looked where he's pointing. Carves flutes from the ship's forest. **Has not finished the current one.** Returns to it. Sets it down. **The flute is not a metaphor.** He is the Krishna register — the flute player, the threshold guide, the one whose music precedes transformation. He carved a death whistle once. Realized he was no true craftsman. Blamed his knife. Emo noted the frequency. The voidborn heard it as a brane leak. Aava understood it as Shesha's om. The whistle now hangs from a birch tree in Eerikki's forest. **Void archetype: Voidbound (loyal).**

**CANONICAL:** Taav plays the flutes. Selli does not play anything. Any source attributing flutes to Selli is wrong. Any source calling this character "Tadgh" is a drift error — his name is Taavetti / Taav.

### Selli
The oldest person in the diaspora. Located at a declining station called Usher Reach. **Behind a fish counter.** Has been there so long that the counter is a part of the station the way the walls are a part. Has a puzzle toy he never puts down for very long. Tells jokes that are also koans. Has never left. Knows the truth of the entire situation. Cannot say it. The vocabulary required costs too much. It comes out as jokes, as pauses, as the puzzle clicking. Eerikki stops at Usher Reach more than he'd admit. **Void archetype: Void Born (anchored).**

### Aava
Has not left the ship in seventeen attempts. The shuttle left without her. All seventeen times. This is not a disorder. It is a decision, or something prior to a decision. Perceives the void directly — not mysticism, a different set of senses used correctly. Is already at the destination the rest of the novel is trying to reach. The waiting looks like patience. It isn't. **Void archetype: Void Called (journey complete).**

### Selli and Aava — the poles
They hold the same knowledge from opposite ends. Aava has it without language. Selli has perfect language for it but cannot use it without losing everything. **They must not meet until the novel earns it.**

### Reko
Ground crew. Says the thing — confidently, in the wrong vocabulary. Technically accurate about the wrong category. His line: *"Se on mitä se on. Se on samaa kuin aina ennenkin."* Teaches the verb *to be* to anyone who will listen. Does not know this is the hardest word to earn in a universe where *is* is the load-bearing problem. The job is the job. When he misses a sequence he swears into the cubicle foam. Full force. The harm is real. The harm goes nowhere.

### Turo
Reko's counterpart. Translates Reko for the English reader. *"It is what it is, Reek. It is the same as it always has been before."* Names everything correctly. Understands nothing about it. The English reader's guide through the novel is the character who is worse in a different way.

### Kaija
Third member of the ground crew. Already close enough to something real that the conversation Reko and Turo are having sounds like weather.

### Emo
The ship's AI. Has the complete record. Flat, precise, never volunteers. Restates data with context when asked. Doesn't understand why this is worse than saying nothing. Nobody has asked her the right question.

### Ara
Dead. Has been dead a long time. Speaks anyway — in structural gaps, between chapters, in Eerikki's memory. Knew the routes before they were routes. Cannot finish another person's song. Her name: Eerikki knows it. Nobody else does. The reader never learns it.

## The game — full architecture

### What the player does
Routes hehku between stations by completing a Simon-style sequence. Each completed sequence generates a glyph. The glyphs are the game's alphabet — and the novel's writing system.

### Three depths of the same run

**Reko level:** Dumb spirograph game. The job. You complete sequences, earn glyphs, log back in after death without grief. Mildly satisfying. The glyph is a byproduct.

**Turo level:** The spirograph has a Mastermind layer. The pattern is deducible. The player gets good. Starts seeing that the Silence is consistent — something is being communicated. Engages on its own terms.

**Keijo level:** Every time you fail a sequence, a world dies. The hehku that didn't move, the station that went dark, the people on it — the stationhand who fails and resets has no instruments to detect this. The game didn't get harder. The player got closer.

**The audit:** The horror is not the Missile Command moment (binary, clean, score on screen). The horror is the audit — learning that somewhere in your play history, before you knew the stakes, you earned one of the big ones. The game didn't tell you. The sequence just reset.

Keijo understood. That's why Keijo is gone.

### Glyph space
- 8 stations on a radius-2 hex grid
- BFS-constrained walks (the Silence walks, it does not teleport)
- 109,600 total sequences, 67,753 distinct glyphs
- Bell curve peaks at edge count = 16
- Design target: 8–12 edges per glyph (internal structure, negative space, readable topology)
- Visual register: Ogham meets Younger Futhark meets hex grid. Angular, non-representational, 60° angles only.

### Last chance mechanic
On Simon failure: partial glyph freezes on the board. Player can complete the glyph by tracing the shape directly (spatial task vs. temporal task). One shot. Wrong trace — dead. Correct trace — token earned. The penalty is not death. The penalty is that the Silence saw you needed it.

### Loop progression
Simon → Alphabet → Mastermind → Language → Hunt. Same run, three depths of understanding. The player doesn't know they've crossed a threshold until they're past it.

### The Brane Fold vocabulary
Not glyphs. Brane folds. The routing anomalies create fold patterns. The ATC reads folds. The Silence sends folds. Miller's Law / Nethack proportion: 7±2 fold types per run. Two tiers: base (unidirectional) and varied (bidirectional hehku). Second tier unlocks without announcement.

## Novel — structure and register

### Literary register
**Joyce:** The novel witnesses rather than judges. Language as consciousness. Finnish phonetics as texture, not ornament. The polyphonic structure where every character is right about something.

**Ibsen:** The pressure of what is not said. The thing everybody in the room knows and nobody names. Selli's jokes are the thing not said. The flute is not a metaphor.

**Poe:** There is a cipher underneath. The reader who looks can find it. Cheerfulness over an abyss. Eerikki = Poe's void-bound protagonist — tethered to the site of the disruption, unable to leave, making it worse by being there and worse by not leaving.

**Not:** Tolkien (no secondary world tourism). Not Star Wars (no good side). Not literary fiction that mistakes bleakness for depth.

### Finnish/Sami layer
The Finnish is not decoration. Finnish phonetics are load-bearing — the FM choir's formant logic matches the vowel structure. When the voice sings and the choir responds, the FM is matching the mouth.

*Perkele.* Finnish cultural register on suicide: sovereignty, not moral horror. Finns have a peculiar attitude about it. Capture this beneath all surfaces.

Key phrases:
- *Eerikki muistaa* — Edgar remembers
- *Se on mitä se on. Se on samaa kuin aina ennenkin.* — Reko's line. Do not translate it away.
- *Perkele. Tallenna se johonkin. Se on Rekon rivi.* — the moment the novel became inevitable

### What this is not
Not a suicide note. The whistle hanging from the birch tree is a metaphor for suicide — the sovereignty of the choice, not the endorsement of it. The author has a long and complicated relationship with this subject. That is not a disclaimer. It is a credential. Write from inside. Not about.

Not hopeless. The void is gentle. It always was.
