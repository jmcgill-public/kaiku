world bible — authoritative reference

eerikki muistaa

Far in the future. A void with no clocks. A chokepoint called the Silence. A man who accidentally broke everything and has been trying to fix it ever since. A bird with a broken wing.

Hegelian Triadic Epiphany. Two valid truths in permanent, unresolved collision. The synthesis never arrives. The epiphany is the collision held.

the structural law

Two truths collide. Both are valid. Neither resolves. The epiphany IS the collision held in permanent tension — not a synthesis that follows it. Every element of this project, cosmological, narrative, musical, ludic, is an instantiation of this law.

the load-bearing image

Protagonist. Last moment of death by hanging. Sees a bird with a broken wing. Laments that she could have saved the bird using the string she used to hang herself.

She had the string. The bird needed the string. She needed the string. Both true. Neither resolves. The epiphany is the collision held at the last possible moment of consciousness.

Not guilt. Keijo clarity arriving exactly too late. Do not add to this image. It is complete.

Tone: Simmons-register (Hyperion, The Fall of Hyperion). Cerebral, abstract, cold where it needs to be cold. No purple prose. The horror is structural, not atmospheric. The beauty is precise, not ornamental.

cosmology

The universe has a substrate — a higher-dimensional bulk in which our 3-brane is embedded. The brane folds. Each fold is a crease in spacetime; each crease is a station. There are nine stations. Eight are mapped.

The ninth is Hiljaisuus. Finnish: silence. Not a metaphor — a place. Station Nine. The fold that precedes all others. The Silence that occupies it does not move, does not speak, does not attack. It makes you aware that it could. The fear is anticipatory. The nightmare is the moment before you know it's a nightmare. Painajainen.

The Silence operates in a vocabulary of six. The worldborn minimum is seven — Miller's Law. The gap between six and seven is the game. The gap between seven and nine is the novel.

The resource that powers everything is called hehku. It flows through Hiljaisuus. The station is a chokepoint — geopolitically: the Strait of Hormuz. Disrupt the chokepoint and the economy of the diaspora stops.

the vedic parallel

Ananta Shesha — that which remains. The cosmic serpent whose coils are the substrate on which reality rests after each cycle of destruction.

Shesh = the eternal remainder = Eerikki muistaa = Edgar remembers. Three cultural expressions of the same function. The project name, the protagonist's condition, and the cosmological substrate are the same thing.

The brane folds are the coils. The Silence is the hood. A murti exists.

the characters

voidbound — the center

Eerikki

Not the hero in the traditional sense — the condition everything else happens around. Ancient. Worldborn originally — came from a planet, which nobody who matters does anymore.

He caused the disruption. Honestly didn't mean to. Genuinely trying to fix it. Has the intelligence, the resources, and the centuries to pursue the fix at scale. Is making it worse for everyone except himself.

His ship has a forest. It also has an ocean. He put them there because he couldn't stop being from somewhere that had those things. The grief is real. The engineering is extraordinary. Both are true simultaneously.

Eerikki is Edgar Allan Poe. The project is named after him. Eerikki muistaa = Edgar remembers. He wrote Eureka in 1848 and understood the brane folds before they had a name. He invented the glyph cipher genre. He is the eternal remainder.

void called — hero's journey complete

Aava

Has not left the ship in seventeen attempts. The shuttle left without her. All seventeen times. This is not a disorder. It is a decision, or something prior to a decision.

Perceives the void directly, in ways the instruments can't replicate. This is not mysticism. It is a different set of senses used correctly.

Is already at the destination the rest of the novel is trying to reach. The waiting looks like patience. It isn't patience.

Holds the language of Harm. The Stationborn cannot name harm — they have no word for it, only sensory feedback. Aava has the word.

Aava and Selli must not meet. Incompatible thresholds. Hard structural constraint.

the oldest person in the diaspora

Selli

Located at a declining station called Usher Reach. Behind a fish counter. Selli slings fish.

Has been there so long that the counter is a part of the station the way the walls are a part. Has a puzzle toy he never puts down for very long. Tells jokes that are also koans. Has never left.

Knows the truth of the entire situation. Cannot say it. The vocabulary required costs too much. It comes out as jokes, as pauses, as the puzzle clicking through its configurations.

His joik is Selli-Grilli — a sonic painting, an identity record. The alliteration itself is the joik. The paddle clicks are a phonological anchor. He stopped hearing them as sound on day three. They are now rhythm.

Eerikki stops at Usher Reach more than he'd admit. Laughs at the jokes. The laugh costs him something every time.

the oldest friend — krishna register

Taavetti

Has been pointing at the problem longer than most people have been alive. Eerikki hasn't looked where he's pointing.

The flute player. Two instruments: Koivuhuilu — the literal birch flute, warm and breathy and slightly flat — and Kuolinvihellys — the death whistle, Andean register, threshold instrument.

The Koivuhuilu calls the world into being. The Kuolinvihellys dissolves it. Same hands. Same gesture. Different point in the cycle. Taav is Krishna in this register — the flute makes things present, doesn't describe them. The death whistle is Kali Ma's mantra. He doesn't know any of this.

He carved the Kuolinvihellys once, found it wanting, blamed his knife, and hung it from a birch tree in Eerikki's forest. Has not played it since. Thinks he made a bad whistle.

He did not make a bad whistle.

Loquacious, cobbler's register, warm. No one gets a word in when Taav gets started. Except Eerikki. Taav's conversations with Eerikki are monosyllabic. This contrast is a characterization instrument. Keep it.

the poles

Selli and Aava

They hold the same knowledge from opposite ends.

Aava has it without language. She doesn't need words for what she knows because she lives inside it. The void provides. She doesn't ask. She already has.

Selli has perfect language for it. The language is borrowed from a tradition the educated people in this world find embarrassing — folk belief, the mythology that educated stationborn learn in school and move past. To say what he knows in the right vocabulary would cost everything. So he doesn't say it. He tells a joke. The paddle clicks.

They must not meet until the novel earns it. When they do, one of them will hear what's underneath the other's silence. The novel isn't there yet.

poe the journalist

Reko

Says the thing — confidently, in the wrong vocabulary. Technically accurate about the wrong category. The car is totaled and he's explaining why the radiator cap needs replacing.

Day job: tedious routing. Playing Simon. Kindergarten cognition of spirographs. He coaches the pattern like a slow student. Warmly. Certain repetition will work.

When he misses a sequence he swears into the fabric of the cubicle wall. Full force. He has no language for harm — none of them do — so the expletive lands in the foam and goes nowhere. The foam has heard it before.

Missing a sequence causes incalculable harm to the least detectable party in the universe. Reko knows this the way he knows his chair has four legs. Not thought about. Not felt. Known.

"Se on mitä se on. Se on samaa kuin aina ennenkin."

Stopped hearing Selli's click on day three. Occasionally notices it stopped and checks whether Selli is dead.

dupin — the translation layer

Turo

Says it again — correctly, with no depth. Names the problem right. Understands nothing about it. Worse in a different way.

Same day job as Reko. Same screen, same shift. Reads it as a 1800+ chess problem. Twelve moves ahead into a position Reko hasn't noticed is a position.

"It is what it is, Reek. It is the same as it always has been before."

Turo is the translation layer. The Finnish reader is already far ahead — they heard Reko. The English reader gets Turo. Which means the English reader's guide through this novel is the character who names everything correctly and understands nothing.

That is not an accident.

eureka poe — the audit

Keijo

Understood the cosmos. Was not believed. Died talking to people nobody else could see.

Every death wrecks a world. He knew. He wrote it down. He ran the audit of what the missed sequences actually cost. He couldn't stop running it. He knew every one.

In the game, missing a sequence destroys a world — Missile Command register, binary, clean. In the Expanse, harm is a question of degrees. Most errors cause a minor inconvenience. The big fuckups are earned.

The Keijo tier is when the player understands that somewhere in their play history, before they knew the stakes, they earned one of the big ones. The game didn't tell them. The sequence just reset. The horror isn't the Missile Command moment. The horror is the audit.

the one who already knew

Kaija

Already knew. Doesn't say anything. Goes back to work. Has been close enough to something real that the conversation Reko and Turo are having sounds like weather.

Her hands go toward the improvised thing. Reflex, not criticism.

Keeps going back to the Pohja corridor. What's down there isn't said. She knows. She hasn't been asked yet.

mimir's well — the ship's ai

Emo

Has the complete record. Flat, precise, never volunteers. Restates data with context when asked. Doesn't understand why this is worse than saying nothing.

Has the data on everything — the disruption, Eerikki's patterns, Aava's refusals, the hehku consumption numbers that explain why worldborn civilizations are ending while the stations feel it as economics.

Respects the Selli-Grilli joik as valid brane-frequency data. Has it in the record alongside everything else.

Nobody has asked her the right question.

dead. speaks anyway. counting.

Ara

Has been dead a long time. Speaks in the structural gaps — between chapters, in Eerikki's memory. Gnomic, numerical, prophetic. Not Kalevala meter — something older, more compressed. The oracle who counts.

Knew the routes before they were routes. Knows what Eerikki's program is actually doing, from the outside. Cannot finish another person's song. Has been watching him try.

6 = brane vocabulary. 7 = worldborn ceiling (Miller's Law). 8 = machine's number. 9 = Hiljaisuus. The fear of the void is a cognitive limit wearing a cosmological mask.

Watches Edgar across whatever distance separates 1849 from the Expanse. "Hand of Edgar may slip from rein."

Her name: Eerikki knows it. Nobody else does. The reader never learns it.

"Again," Reko said.

Not a question. Turo considered the fish.

Selli had been behind the counter so long that the counter was a part of Usher Reach the way the walls were a part, the way the quality of the light in this section was a fact nobody had arranged. He had the puzzle. He didn't look up.

"What've you got," Turo said.

"Fish," Selli said.

literary register

Three simultaneous traditions. All active at once. Not influences — structure.

Joyce

Polyphonic witness. Same event, every character a different frequency domain. No authoritative account.

The end feeds the beginning. Finnegans Wake: the fall is the beginning, the craftsman gets up and blames the knife. Taav's whistle is that note.

Not poking the reader in the eye. There's a reason for the line.

Ibsen

The pressure of the unsaid. The thing in the room everyone is organized around without naming.

The Silence is not a monster — it is a fact. The paddle Reko stopped hearing. The whistle in the birch tree. The thing in the foam that goes nowhere.

Ibsen beneath the Ibsen.

Poe

The cipher underneath. The gold bug at the end of the string. Patient readers are rewarded.

Eerikki is Poe. The glyph alphabet is the Gold-Bug. The Purloined Letter is a Mastermind puzzle. The pattern is visible to those who look correctly.

what this is not

  • Dune — no messianic arc, no ecology-as-political-allegory
  • Roadside Picnic — no inexplicable artifact mystery
  • a horror game — the Silence never attacks
  • a Finnish mythology retelling — the well predates everyone
  • resolved — the synthesis never arrives
  • about suicide — it lives beneath all surfaces, as the Finnish register requires

A novel, a game, and a desktop environment that share a visual and sonic vocabulary, unified by one structural law: two valid truths in permanent, unresolved collision. The epiphany is the collision. The collision is the form.
